What data does Spotify offer?

Spotify offers a lot of great data - everything from song length to the start and stop time of each measure. Here are a few important features to understand:

Loudness - The overall loudness of a track in decibels (dB). Loudness values are averaged across the entire track and are useful for comparing relative loudness of tracks.

Energy - Represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. For example, death metal has high energy, while a Bach prelude scores low on the scale.

Danceability - Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity.

Beats per minute - A calculated field that takes the total number of beats in a song, and divides the count by the length of the song (in minutes).

Drawing Conclusions


Given the track diversity within each of the Arctic Monkeys' recent albums, we can expect that trend to continue into the future. When taking a look at the recommended tracks, you can see the diversity of values across all 5 Spotify features, but a trend among those tracks is high score in loudness, Energy and Danceability, with a lower score in Beats per Minutes and Song Length.


This suggests a possible return to the sounds of their debut album Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not, which featured loud, fast-paced music. This is best examplified by the track Red Light Indicates Doors Are Secured, available below.
